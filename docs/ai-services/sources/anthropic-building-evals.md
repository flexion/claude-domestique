## Evaluation Structure

Evals comprise four essential components:
1. **Input prompt** — Variables fed into a prompt template
2. **Model output** — Completion generated by Claude
3. **Golden answer** — Reference standard for comparison (exact match or exemplar)
4. **Score** — Grade from one of three grading methods

## Grading Methodologies

### Code-based grading
Uses string matching and regex patterns. Fastest and most reliable but only works for structured outputs. "Code-based grading is the best grading method if possible, as it is fast and highly reliable."

### Human grading
Manual evaluation by people. Most capable but slow and expensive — a last resort. The golden answer becomes instruction guidelines rather than strict answers.

### Model-based grading
Leverages Claude to evaluate responses via a "grader prompt." Can grade open-ended answers using rubric-based correctness tags.

## Golden Answer

A golden answer serves as ground truth — "a mandatory exact match, or an example of a perfect answer meant to give a grader a point of comparison." Golden answers serve dual purposes: as exact-match targets in code-based systems, or as "instructions on what to look for" when humans or models grade subjective responses.

## Key Guidance

"Grading is a cost you will incur every time you re-run your eval, in perpetuity" — making automation prioritized over manual review. Best practices: design automatable evals through clever structure, prioritize question volume over individual quality, and test model-based graders empirically before deployment.
